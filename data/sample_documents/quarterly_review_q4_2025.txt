Quarterly Business Review — Q4 2025

Prepared by: Office of the CFO
Date: January 15, 2026

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE

Total revenue for Q4 2025 reached $127.3M, exceeding the forecast of $118M by 7.9%.
Gross margin improved to 64.2% from 61.8% in Q3, driven by operational efficiencies
in the supply chain and renegotiated vendor contracts.

Operating expenses increased 4.1% quarter-over-quarter to $62.4M, primarily due to
headcount additions in engineering (+18 FTEs) and sales (+12 FTEs).

EBITDA margin: 28.7% (target: 26.0%)
Free cash flow: $31.2M (target: $24.0M)
Net revenue retention: 118% (target: 110%)

PRODUCT HIGHLIGHTS

The Enterprise Analytics Platform (EAP) v3.2 shipped on schedule in November,
contributing $8.4M in incremental ARR. Customer adoption metrics show 73% of
enterprise accounts activated the new predictive insights module within 30 days.

The AI Assistant feature (beta) received positive feedback from 92% of pilot
participants. Full GA is planned for March 2026.

CUSTOMER METRICS

Total customers: 2,847 (+142 net new in Q4)
Enterprise accounts (>$100K ARR): 312 (+28 net new)
Average contract value: $184K (up from $167K in Q3)
Churn rate: 2.1% (improved from 2.8% in Q3)
NPS: 67 (up from 62 in Q3)

WORKFORCE

Total headcount: 1,243 (up from 1,213)
Voluntary attrition: 3.2% annualized
Diversity: 41% women in leadership roles (target: 45% by end of 2026)
Employee engagement score: 4.2/5.0

OUTLOOK FOR Q1 2026

Management expects Q1 revenue between $130M and $135M, with continued margin
expansion as cloud migration savings begin to materialize. The APAC launch
in Singapore is on track for late February.

Key risks include potential tariff impacts on hardware procurement and
competitive pressure from CloudNext's new enterprise offering launched in December.
